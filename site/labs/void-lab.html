Void Lab

Aleph breath study for resetting the vesica field before entering charged chapels.

Orientation

The Void Lab offers a silent vestibule where the nervous system may soften before deeper practice. Geometry stays implied through soft vesica overlays rather than animated mandalas (why: keeps the space ND-safe).

The Aleph current invites three slow breath sets. Each set traces a vertical line, a horizontal span, and finally the vesica curve, mirroring the Cosmic Helix renderer's first layer.

Preparation

  1. Dim the surrounding lights to a single soft source. Aim for 33–40 lux so gradients remain readable without glare.
  2. Open the Cosmic Helix renderer in a separate window. Allow your gaze to rest on the vesica grid for three breaths.
  3. Arrange journal tools or tactile objects within reach. Keep edges rounded; avoid sharp metallic implements.
If stimulation rises above a calm threshold, slide the Calm Mode toggle to mute gradients. The body leads the session cadence.

Practice Sequence

  1. Three Breath Gate: Inhale for a count of 7, hold for 3, release for 11. Visualise the breath aligning with the vesica's vertical axis.
  2. Listening Sweep: Trace your attention across the horizontal line, noticing texture shifts in sound or temperature. Record any notes in brief phrases.
  3. Mandorla Presence: Allow awareness to settle in the central vesica overlap. Whisper one anchor word (e.g., 'still', 'open').

Repeat the sequence in sets of 3. Between sets, sip water or rest your eyes. No timers, no chimes.

Integration & Notes

  • Sketch the vesica or jot a single sentence describing the body's signal after each set.
  • Optional: transcribe the most resonant word into the Aleph column of your codex.
  • Close by thanking the silence; ground with a small snack or gentle stretch.
